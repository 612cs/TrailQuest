Hiking and other outdoor sports are becoming increasingly popular, attracting many new users.
These beginners no longer satisfy themselves with simple route information; they place more
emphasis on whether the route is suitable for their level, whether the weather is appropriate,
and whether they can enjoy unique landscapes. However, there are many problems with the map
navigation tools on the market, mainly manifested in incomplete track data, insufficiently timely
weather information updates, and recommendation functions that are mostly sorted based on
popularity. This article designs and implements an intelligent route recommendation system for
outdoor hiking scenarios to address these issues. The system uses a front-end and back-end
separation architecture, with the front end using Vue 3 and TypeScript, and the back end
utilizing Spring Boot 3, MySQL, and Redis for business processing and interface services. The
system integrates DeepSeek, a large language model, in the intelligent service, allowing users to
express their travel needs in natural language, and the system matches and recommends routes
accordingly.This article uses a random forest model to predict the probability of the appearance
of unique landscapes such as sea of clouds, hoar frost, ice formations, and sunrise and sunset,
thereby providing environmental assistance information for travel decisions. The platform has
achieved the design goals in its main functions such as route search, intelligent dialogue
recommendation, weather query, and landscape prediction, which can significantly reduce the cost
of information collection for users before their trips.
